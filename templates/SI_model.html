SIR Model

In addition to ARIMA model, one of the machine learning methods, we also built a SIR model to forecast the spread of COVID-19. SIR model is one of the most representative compartmental models in epidemiology. It calculates the hypothetical number of infectious people over time considering removed infectious cases and assuming the total number of population remain constant. SIR model has three main components:

S: number of susceptible people
I: number number of infectious people
R: number of removed infectious people

In this webpage, we allow users to adjust the two parameters of SIR Model: Beta and Gamma.

Beta (β) is the infectious rate. According the theory (from wiki), Beta (β) is the average number of contacts per person per time, multiplied by the probability of disease transmission in a contact between a susceptible and an infectious subject. The higher the beta, the faster the COVID-19 could transmit. The other parameter Gamma (γ) is the recovery rate. 1/ γ is the infectious

Based on your expectation, you can adjust Beta and Gamma parameters. After taking effective public health intervention methods such as wearing masks, keeping social distance, washing hands, increasing vaccination coverage, we can lower the infectious rate (beta) and reduce the spread of COVID-19. With enhanced medical treatment solutions, we can shorter the recovery period and improve Gamma ratio.

Feel free to play with Beta and Gamma to create your own SIR model. What you need to do is to input a Beta and a recovery date (1/γ). You can try different assumptions to see how COVID-19 spread will change over time.
